Classic Mango Milkshake

A refreshing mango milkshake is a perfect treat, especially in warmer weather! Here's a simple recipe to make one:




Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe mangoes (about 1.5 to 2 cups chopped mango)
  • 1 to 1.5 cups chilled milk (dairy or a dairy-free alternative like almond, soy, or coconut milk)
  • 1-2 tablespoons sugar, honey, or your preferred sweetener (adjust to taste, depending on the sweetness of your mangoes)
  • 3-4 ice cubes (optional, for extra chill and thickness)
  • Optional Garnishes
    • Chopped almonds or pistachios
    • Whipped Cream
    • A pinch of ground cardamom or saffron strands
    • A slice of fresh mango or mint leaves

    Instructions:

  1. Prepare the mangoes:

    Wash, peel, and chop the mangoes into small pieces. Discard the pit.

  2. Blend:

    Add the chopped mango pieces to a blender.

  3. Add liquids and sweetener:

    Pour in the chilled milk and add your chosen sweetener. If you're using ice cubes, add them now.

  4. Blend until smooth:

    Blend on high speed until the mixture is completely smooth and creamy, with no mango chunks remaining. If you prefer a thinner milkshake, add a little more milk and blend again. For a thicker shake, use less milk or use frozen mango chunks.

  5. Taste and adjust:

    Taste the milkshake and adjust the sweetness if needed.

  6. Serve:

    Pour the mango milkshake into serving glasses.

  7. Garnish (optional):

    Garnish with your desired toppings like chopped nuts, whipped cream, or a sprinkle of cardamom.

  8. Enjoy immediately!

    Mango milkshakes are best served fresh.

Tips for the Best Mango Milkshake:

  • Choose ripe mangoes:

    The key to a delicious mango milkshake is using sweet, ripe mangoes. Alphonso, Kesar, Sindhri, or Dasheri varieties are often recommended for their flavor and less fibrous texture.

  • Chill your ingredients:

    Using chilled milk and pre-chilled or even frozen mango pieces will result in a colder and thicker milkshake

  • Adjust sweetness:

    The amount of sweetener needed will depend on how sweet your mangoes are. Start with a small amount and add more as desired.

  • For extra richness:

    Add a scoop of vanilla or mango ice cream to the blender for a richer, creamier milkshake. If you do this, you might need less sugar.

  • Flavor variations:

    A tiny pinch of ground cardamom or a few saffron strands can add a lovely aromatic touch.

Enjoy your delicious mango milkshake! It's a perfect way to cool down and enjoy the tropical flavors of mangoes.

Nutrition Information (per serving, approx.):

  • Calories: 250-300 (depending on the amount of sweetener and milk used)
  • Protein: 6-8g
  • Carbohydrates: 45-50g
  • Fat: 5-10g (if using whole milk or adding ice cream)
  • Fiber: 2-3g
  • Vitamins: Rich in Vitamin A and C from the mangoes
  • Minerals: Contains calcium (from milk) and potassium (from mangoes)

Note: Nutrition values can vary based on the specific ingredients and quantities used.

Storage:

Mango milkshakes are best enjoyed fresh, but if you have leftovers, you can store them in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours. Stir well before consuming, as separation may occur. For longer storage, consider freezing the milkshake in an airtight container for up to a month. Thaw in the refrigerator and blend again before serving.

Variations:

  • Vegan Mango Milkshake:

    Use coconut milk or almond milk instead of dairy milk and a plant-based sweetener.

  • Spiced Mango Milkshake:

    Add a pinch of cinnamon or nutmeg for a warm spice flavor.

  • Chocolate Mango Milkshake:

    Add a tablespoon of cocoa powder or chocolate syrup for a chocolatey twist.

  • Green Mango Milkshake:

    Use unripe mangoes for a tangy version, adjusting the sweetness accordingly.

  • Protein Mango Milkshake:

    Add a scoop of protein powder for an extra protein boost, making it a great post-workout drink.

Feel free to experiment with these variations to find your perfect mango milkshake recipe!
